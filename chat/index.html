I want to create a mobile app where users can send files from one device to another, regardless of whether they have internet access
There are a few ways to create a mobile app that allows users to send files from one device to another without an internet connection. One approach is to use a technology called "peer-to-peer" (P2P) networking. P2P allows devices to connect directly to one another and share files without the need for a central server. Another approach is to use a technology called "Wi-Fi Direct." Wi-Fi Direct allows devices to connect to each other directly over Wi-Fi, without the need for a wireless access point. This can be used to send files from one device to another without an internet connection. You can also use Bluetooth to transfer files between devices. It is also important to consider the security and privacy of the files being transferred, and to implement appropriate measures such as encryption. You may want to consider using existing libraries or frameworks like Alljoyn, ShareIt, etc. to help you create your app.
Here are some additional features that could enhance the user experience of your mobile app:
  1. File transfer progress indicator
    A progress bar or percentage indicator that shows how much of the file has been transferred.
  2. Resumable transfers
    The ability to pause and resume file transfers in case of interruption.
  3. File transfer history
    A feature that keeps a record of all the files that have been transferred through the app, along with the date and time of the transfer.
  4. Push notifications
    Notify users when a file transfer is complete or when a file has been received.
  5. Multi-language support
    The ability to support different languages for the app interface to reach wider audience.
  6. File compression
    Allowing users to compress large files before sending them to save time and bandwidth.
  7. File encryption
    Provide the option to encrypt the files being transferred to keep them secure.
  8. Multi-platform support
    The ability to transfer files between different types of devices and platforms, such as iOS and Android.
  9. Group file sharing
    Allow multiple users to share and access files together.
  10. Cloud integration
    Allow users to transfer files to and from cloud storage services like Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.
These are just a few examples of features that could be added to enhance the user experience of your mobile app. You may find other features that would be useful or relevant to the particular use case of your app.

Do you want help in planning the project?
Here are some steps you can take to plan your mobile app project
  1. Define the project goals
    Clearly define the purpose and objectives of your app. This will help you to stay focused and ensure that your app meets the needs of your target audience.
  2. Conduct market research
    Research the market and your competitors to see what similar apps are available, what features they offer, and how they are marketed. This will help you to identify gaps in the market and determine how your app can be differentiated.
  3. Create a feature list
    Create a list of the features that you want to include in your app. Prioritize the features according to their importance and the needs of your target audience.
  4. Estimate the development time
    Estimate the time required to develop your app, including the design, development, testing, and deployment phases.
  5. Determine the resources required
    Determine the resources required to complete the project, including the development team, equipment, and any other necessary resources.
  6. Create a project schedule
    Create a detailed project schedule that outlines the milestones and deadlines for each phase of the project.
  7. Develop a project budget
    Develop a project budget that includes the costs for development, testing, deployment, and ongoing maintenance.
  8. Choose development method
    Choose the development method that best suits your project, such as agile, waterfall, or Scrum.
  9. Create a testing plan
    Create a testing plan that includes the types of testing that will be performed, such as unit testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing.
  10. Create a deployment plan
    Create a deployment plan that outlines the steps that will be taken to deploy the app on the app store and make it available to users.
